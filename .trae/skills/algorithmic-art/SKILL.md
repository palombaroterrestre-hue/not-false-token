# Algorithmic Art Philosophy

This skill defines a workflow for creating generative art by first establishing a "Philosophy" (the logic/rules) and then describing its "Expression" (the visual output).

## Workflow

1.  **Define Philosophy**: Describe the mathematical or logical rules (e.g., "Organic Turbulence" using Perlin noise).
2.  **Describe Expression**: Detail how the logic manifests visually (colors, shapes, density).

## Examples

**"Organic Turbulence"**
Philosophy: Chaos constrained by natural law, order emerging from disorder.
Algorithmic expression: Flow fields driven by layered Perlin noise. Thousands of particles following vector forces, their trails accumulating into organic density maps.

**"Geometric Recursion"**
Philosophy: Self-similarity across scales, infinite complexity from simple rules.
Algorithmic expression: Recursive subdivision of space. Rectangles splitting into smaller quadrilaterals based on golden ratio proportions, creating a Piet Mondrian-inspired structure with dynamic line weights.
